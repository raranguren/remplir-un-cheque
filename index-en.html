How to write a cheque in France

Unlike other countries where they may be being phased out, cheques are widely used in France. French cheque book is called a «Chéquier» or a «Carnet de chèques», and most banks will give them for free to their customers. A cheque is called a «chèque», they are linked to your bank account, but they are only valid when filled correctly and signed by the owner of the bank account.

Writing a french cheque is simple as long as you follow the steps correctly:

  1. Enter the amount you want to pay
  2. Enter the amount in words
  3. Enter the payee's name
  4. Indicate the place and date
  5. Sign your cheque

1Enter the amount you want to pay

There is a box to the right of the cheque in which you need to put the amount you want to pay. Leaving no space to the left, write all the digits. If the number has decimals, use a comma as the separator. Then fill the rest of the box with a horizontal line to prevent modifications.

2Enter the amount in words

Below the bank logo, the first field reads «Payez contre ce chèque» , which means «pay against this cheque».

You need to input the amount in words, starting right after the above wording and filling the remaining space with a horizontal line.

Payez contre ce chèque quarente-deux euros

Note that most banks accept cheques filled in other languages, even with numbers. However, if someone tampers with the numbers, it is hard to prove the forgery and this guide follows the best practices to avoid such scams.

3Enter the payee's name

The sentence above continues with «à», which means «to».

You need to input the name of the person or company that will receive the payment.

Never sign a cheque without a payee's name. If it is cashed with a different name, you might be unable to prove that you paid a bill and be asked to pay it again.

4Indicate the city and date

Below there is space for your signature. Nearby there are two fields to fill in the place and date.

The place is usually indicated with «À» or «Fait à». It is the city where you are signing the cheque, NOT your address.

The word «le» precedes the date of signature. Write the date in which you will give the cheque to the payee. Use the French format, which is day/month/year, as shown below:

Fait à Paris

le DD/MM/YYYY

Reminder: All cheques are valid for 1 year and 8 days. It is legally prohibited to backdate or postdate them.

5Sign your cheque

Before signing, detach your cheque from the cheque book. This prevents the signature mark on your cheque from showing up on the next cheque, which could allow your signature to be forged if your cheque book is lost or stolen.

The signature must be the same that you registered with the bank, as they will be compared during the verification.
